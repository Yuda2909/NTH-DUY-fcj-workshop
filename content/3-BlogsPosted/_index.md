This section lists and introduces the blogs I posted or prepared during the internship program. These blogs are based on AWS technical articles and focus on summarizing key ideas, reviewing practical use cases, and reflecting on how AWS services are applied in real-world scenarios.

### [Blog 1 - Automating Landing Zone Creation with AWS Transform](3.1-Blog1/)

This blog summarizes and reviews how AWS Transform can help automate the creation of a Landing Zone using AI-powered guidance. Instead of spending several weeks designing and setting up a Landing Zone manually, AWS Transform can assist with account structure design, migration wave planning, and infrastructure code generation using AWS CDK or Landing Zone Accelerator YAML.

The blog also highlights important points such as Human-In-The-Loop approval, brownfield environment scanning, cost considerations, and the complexity of decommissioning a Landing Zone after deployment.

### [Blog 2 - Physical AI in Healthcare: Redefining Care Delivery at the Edge](3.2-Blog2/)

This blog introduces a Physical AI healthcare project demonstrated at Mobile World Congress 2026 through the collaboration between AWS, NVIDIA, and AI-SENSE. The project focuses on bringing hospital-level monitoring and patient care closer to the home through applications such as Virtual Ward and Health Buddy.

The blog explains how AI-native networks, Agentic AI, Digital Twin Simulation, Amazon Bedrock, Amazon SageMaker, AWS Outposts, Amazon EKS Anywhere, and NVIDIA Omniverse can work together to support real-time healthcare monitoring and decision support at the edge.

### [Blog 3 - Automating Medical Record Digitization with Amazon Bedrock Data Automation and AWS HealthLake](3.3-Blog3/)

This blog summarizes and reviews how Amazon Bedrock Data Automation and AWS HealthLake can be combined to automate the digitization of medical records. The solution focuses on extracting data from unstructured healthcare documents, such as scanned medical records, prescriptions, handwritten notes, and PDF files, then converting the extracted information into standardized healthcare data.

The blog highlights the role of Generative AI in data automation, especially in transforming messy and unstructured medical documents into clean, structured, and interoperable data. It also explains how Amazon S3, AWS Lambda, Amazon Bedrock Data Automation, and AWS HealthLake can work together in an event-driven serverless architecture.

The blog also discusses important benefits and challenges, including automatic FHIR data standardization, reduced manual data entry, serverless scalability, healthcare data privacy, compliance requirements, GenAI processing costs, latency, and the need for human validation when working with sensitive medical records.
