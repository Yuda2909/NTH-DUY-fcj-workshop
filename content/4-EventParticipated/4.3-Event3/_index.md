#  Cloud Skills & Confidence

## Event Objectives

- Share practical approaches to learning AWS through hands-on experience.
- Introduce AWS Cloud Quest and Floci as tools to build real cloud skills without cost barriers.
- Highlight the role of confidence in career development, campus life, and community involvement.
- Encourage students to move from passive learning (tutorials, documentation) to active, hands-on practice.

---

## Speaker Lineup

- **Huynh Thai Linh** - First Cloud AI Journey
- **Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu** - First Cloud AI Journey

---

## Key Highlights

### 1. Level Up Your AWS Skills with Cloud Quest and Floci

Huynh Thai Linh shared practical approaches to learning AWS through hands-on experience, focusing on two tools:

- **AWS Cloud Quest**: a gamified learning experience with guided labs, real-world scenarios, and official AWS badges that help learners build cloud skills in a structured way.
- **Floci**: an open-source local AWS emulator that allows developers to experiment, develop, and test AWS workloads locally without worrying about cloud costs or unexpected billing.

The key message was that learning AWS is most effective when combined with hands-on practice — building projects, experimenting with services, and learning from mistakes are what truly help deepen understanding, beyond just watching tutorials or reading documentation.

### 2. Why We Always Need Confidence

Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu explored why confidence is a "must-have" for careers, campus life, and everything in between.

Key points from the talk include:

- Confidence directly affects how students present ideas, network with others, and pursue new opportunities.
- Confidence is not something people are simply born with — it is built through repeated practice, small wins, and stepping outside one's comfort zone.
- A supportive community and mentorship play an important role in helping individuals grow their confidence over time.

---

## Key Takeaways and Personal Reflections

### Learning AWS Through Practice, Not Just Theory

This session reinforced the idea that reading about AWS services is not enough. Tools like Cloud Quest and Floci make it possible to practice in a low-risk, low-cost environment, which lowers the barrier for students to start experimenting with real cloud workloads.

### Confidence as a Foundation Skill

The talk on confidence was a reminder that technical skills alone are not enough. Being able to speak up, share ideas, and take on new challenges — such as delivering a first public talk in front of 200+ people — requires confidence that is built over time through practice and support from mentors and community.

---

## Professional Application Plan

After this event, I can apply the knowledge in the following ways:

- **Use AWS Cloud Quest** to practice cloud concepts in a structured, gamified way.
- **Use Floci** to test AWS workloads locally before deploying to the cloud, reducing cost risk.
- **Prioritize hands-on projects** over passive learning to deepen understanding.
- **Actively build confidence** by taking small opportunities to speak, present, or share knowledge with others.
- **Support and encourage peers** to step out of their comfort zone, the way mentors did for the speakers.

---

## Conclusion

The May 30th event provided valuable lessons in two areas: practical, hands-on AWS learning through Cloud Quest and Floci, and the importance of confidence in both technical and personal growth. It was a reminder that becoming a capable cloud practitioner requires not only technical practice but also the confidence to keep learning, sharing, and growing within the community.
